# DUPS Photo Contest — How to Vote

You only need your phone and the QR code that's projected at the meeting.

## Step by step

1. **Open your phone's camera.** Point it at the QR code on the projector
   screen. A notification pops up — tap it to open the voting page in your
   browser.
2. **Wait** until the Administrator says voting is open. Your screen will
   show "Waiting for the Administrator…" until then.
3. When the Administrator opens voting, your screen changes to **Cast Your
   Vote — Choose a photo from 1 to N**.
4. **Type the number** of the photo you think is best (just the number —
   1, 2, 3, etc.).
5. **Tap Submit Vote**. Done.

Your screen will say "Vote recorded for Photo #N."

---

## Changing your vote

As long as the Administrator hasn't closed voting, **you can change your vote**
as many times as you want. Just type a different number and tap Submit again.
Only your most recent vote counts.

---

## When voting closes

When the Administrator announces voting is closed, your screen says
"Voting Closed. Your final vote: N." That's normal. You're done.

---

## If something goes wrong

**My phone went to sleep and I lost the page.**
Tap the page in your browser history or scan the QR again. Your vote is
saved.

**The page says "Could not reach the server."**
Check your phone's WiFi or cellular signal. The app will reconnect
automatically when the network comes back.

**I scanned the QR but it says "This QR code has already been used."**
The QR was rotated, or someone scanned the same QR earlier from your same
device. Look at the projector — there's a current QR code shown. Scan
that one. If it still fails, ask the Administrator to rotate the QR.

**The page says "The Administrator has locked the room. No new voters
can join."**
You arrived after the host locked the joiner list. Ask the
Administrator to unlock it (they have a button for this).

---

## Why doesn't the app show me the photos?

The photos are displayed in the meeting room — on the projector, on
prints, or however the Administrator chose. The voting app only
collects numbers. This keeps photo quality high (no compression for
phone screens) and keeps the focus on viewing the photos together.

---

## Privacy

- Your vote is anonymous. The app does not record your name, phone number,
  email, or any personal identifier.
- The app uses one cookie to remember that *this device* has voted in
  *this session*. The cookie disappears when the session ends.
- No analytics, no third-party trackers, no ads.
